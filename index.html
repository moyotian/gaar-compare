JGN 2020–25 → 2025–30: Total revenue increased by $930.8M (+42.8%) to $3,106.7M. Driven by a significantly higher WACC (~6.4% vs ~4.3%) lifting return on capital by $481.7M (+63.8%); $115M approved accelerated depreciation increasing the regulatory depreciation block by $74.2M (+16.7%); and opex up $71.9M (+6.1%). Revenue adjustments shifted from –$184.5M (net under-recovery carryover) to +$40.0M. Overview pp. 4–8
Evoenergy 2021–26 → 2026–31: Total revenue increased by $95.4M (+30.1%) to $412.8M. Return on capital rose $39.4M (+46.6%) on higher WACC. Regulatory depreciation grew $36.6M (+73.1%) reflecting the $30M in accelerated depreciation approved through shortening HP/MP pipeline asset lives to align with the ACT Government's 2045 gas network decommissioning target. An additional $35M of accelerated depreciation proposed by Evoenergy beyond reduced asset lives was not accepted. Overview pp. 3–7
AGN SA 2021–26 → 2026–31: Total revenue increased by $248.1M (+22.1%) to $1,370.3M. Return on capital surged $292.1M (+70.6%) as the capital base grew — boosted by the $250M accelerated depreciation in 2021–26 which elevated the opening RAB and by higher forecast capex. Regulatory depreciation fell sharply from $322.9M to $166.1M as the large one-off mains replacement accelerated depreciation is replaced by a small $29M (reduced asset lives only). The opex building block grew by $121.8M (+31.2%). Overview pp. 4–9
AusNet 2018–22 → 2023–28: Total revenue increased by $230.2M (+22.1%) from $1,040.1M to $1,270.3M. Return on capital rose $62.1M (+12.3%) as the capital base grew from ongoing capex; it is now 44.7% of total. Regulatory depreciation grew $96.3M (+52.0%), driven by the $105M approved accelerated depreciation in 2023–28 (nil in 2018–22). No accelerated depreciation was approved in the 2018–22 period. Capex fell from $477.5M to $427.6M as declining demand reduced growth capex. Opex rose from $272.3M to $349.8M ($+77.5M, +28.5%) partly due to addition of abolishment costs ($14.5M) not present in 2018–22. Overview pp. 5–9
Multinet 2018–22 → 2023–28: Total revenue increased by $119.4M (+11.5%) to $1,154.9M. Return on capital rose $96.7M (+26.2%) as capital base grew from ongoing mains replacement and $53M approved accelerated depreciation. Opex building block grew $82.6M (+19.9%) in nominal terms, partly reflecting growing connection abolishment costs from customer departures. Overview pp. 5–10
AGN Vic 2018–22 → 2023–28: Total revenue increased by $168.6M (+14.1%) from $1,193.5M to $1,362.1M. Return on capital grew $89.1M (+17.8%) as the capital base increased from the large ongoing capex program and $175M in approved accelerated depreciation. Regulatory depreciation grew $50.8M (+21.5%) reflecting the accelerated depreciation approved in 2023–28 (nil in 2018–22, where mains replacement was handled as standard regulatory depreciation). Capex fell significantly from $549.6M to $433.5M as the mains replacement program approaches completion. Revenue adjustments of –$93.0M in 2023–28 are the largest negative adjustment across all 12 decisions reviewed, reflecting significant over-recovery carryover from 2018–22. Overview pp. 4–8

Key Commentary — Current Period
JGN 2025–30 ($717.4M, $2024–25): AER approved $120.7M below JGN's revised proposal. Key reductions: renewable gas connections excluded (–$78.9M; AER found insufficient justification); meter replacement cut by $49.7M. Customer connections capex increased by $35.0M net of contributions, based on the AER's higher demand forecast than JGN's. Overview pp. 6–7, 18–19
Evoenergy 2026–31 ($31.4M, $2025–26): Capex is 49.2% below the AER-approved 2021–26 amount, reflecting sharply declining ACT demand. Meter replacement dominates at $15.2M (48% of total) as the network ages; connections capex has fallen to just $0.3M net. Network overheads ($12.6M, 40%) are substantially fixed costs that do not scale with demand reduction. Overview pp. 21–23
AGN SA 2026–31 ($332.3M, $2025–26): AER approved $4.9M (–1.5%) below AGN's revised proposal, cutting ICT by $4.3M and meter replacement by $2.5M. The major SA mains replacement program is substantially complete; mains replacement capex falls to $85.5M (25.7% of total) vs a larger program in 2021–26. New connections at $13.9M net reflects the decline in new SA gas customers. Overview pp. 20–22
AusNet 2023–28 ($427.6M, $2022–23): Accepted as per revised proposal; 14% ($71.7M) below AusNet's actual capex in 2018–22. New connections ($187M) and augmentation ($19.8M) are lower due to declining demand. Safety-driven capex — mains replacement completion, IT and meter replacement — continues at regulated standards irrespective of demand. Overview pp. 24–26
Multinet 2023–28 ($668.7M, $2022–23): Accepted as per revised proposal; 51% ($225.4M) above Multinet's actual capex in 2018–22. Dominated by the mains replacement program at $408M ($2023–24 real, 61% of total capex) to meet Australian Standards and Energy Safe Victoria gas safety case obligations. Multinet's dense urban Melbourne network has aging metal mains requiring systematic replacement. Overview pp. 24–26
AGN Vic 2023–28 ($433.5M, $2022–23): Accepted as per revised proposal; 31% ($196.8M) below AGN Vic's actual capex in 2018–22. New connections ($166.1M) and augmentation ($57.8M) are lower reflecting demand decline. Ongoing mains replacement completion, IT and meter replacement are safety-driven and broadly demand-independent in the near term. Overview pp. 24–26

The depreciation building block comprises two components: (1) Standard regulatory depreciation — return of capital calculated using standard (straight-line) asset lives applied to the approved capital base; and (2) Accelerated depreciation — an additional approved amount to address stranded asset risk by returning capital to investors faster, at the cost of higher near-term prices for customers. Accelerated depreciation is determined under rule 89 of the National Gas Rules.

Key Commentary
JGN 2025–30 — $115M approved (vs $230M proposed): The AER approved $115M accelerated depreciation, halving JGN's $230M proposal. Of the approved $115M: $77M comes from shortened economic lives for certain asset classes; $38M comes from a reduction in the capital base. The AER considered $115M strikes an appropriate balance — providing a meaningful start to managing stranded asset risk while avoiding unnecessarily large price increases. The AER expects to revisit the appropriate level in future periods as demand trajectory becomes clearer. Overview pp. 7, 24–26
Evoenergy 2026–31 — $30M approved (vs $65M proposed): The AER approved $30M through shortened asset lives for HP and MP pipeline assets (to 19 years, aligned to ACT Government's 2045 gas network decommissioning target under the Integrated Energy Plan). An additional $35M proposed by Evoenergy beyond the reduced-asset-lives approach was not accepted — the AER found that reduced asset lives already addressed residual stranded asset risk materially, and that further accelerated depreciation would cause additional upward price pressure (~8.6% p.a. real network price increase) without sufficient justification. Overview pp. 5–6, 24–27
AGN SA 2021–26 — $250M approved: The largest single accelerated depreciation amount in this comparison, approved to recover the net book value of replaced mains and inlets from the major SA mains replacement program. This reflects the retirement of assets from service — a clear trigger for accelerated depreciation. The approved amount was lower than AGN's initial proposal, reflecting the AER's assessment of the quantum of eligible mains replacement and corrections to input errors in AGN's depreciation model. Overview pp. 34–36
AGN SA 2026–31 — $29M approved (vs $70M proposed): A dramatic fall from $250M in 2021–26, with the mains replacement program substantially complete. The $29M approved reflects only reduced asset lives for the Mains and Inlets asset classes (to 30 and 50 years respectively). AGN proposed $70M, arguing this was the minimum viable amount; the AER did not accept this, finding the reduced asset lives approach sufficient to address residual stranded asset risk at lower cost to consumers. Overview pp. 7–8, 22–24
AusNet 2023–28 — $105M approved (vs $200M proposed): The AER described this as a "measured start" to accelerated depreciation for AusNet's Victorian distribution network. The $105M reflects a proportionate response to stranded asset risk while maintaining price affordability for consumers. AusNet's $200M proposal was found to be larger than necessary at this stage, given uncertainty about the pace of gas demand decline. The AER noted it would monitor demand trajectory and revisit in future reviews. Overview pp. 6–7, 22–24
Multinet 2023–28 — $53M approved (vs $86M proposed): Similarly described as a "measured start" to accelerated depreciation for Multinet's aging Melbourne network. The $53M balances addressing stranded asset risk against price impacts for remaining customers. The AER's reduction ($33M from MGN's proposal) reflects the same approach applied to AusNet — deferring a larger step-change in accelerated depreciation until the demand trajectory is clearer. Overview pp. 6–7, 22–24
AGN Vic 2023–28 — $175M approved (as proposed): The largest accelerated depreciation approved in the current Victorian round, and the only Victorian decision where the proposal was accepted in full (the draft decision also accepted $175M). This reflects AGN's extensive Victorian network and the assessed stranded asset risk level. The approval without reduction (unlike AusNet and Multinet) may reflect AGN's stronger supporting evidence base for the proposed amount. Overview pp. 6, 22–24
